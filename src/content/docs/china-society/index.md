# China Society

China’s social debates often surface through internet phrases: class solidification, downward mobility, K-shaped recovery, disappearing ladders, exam pressure, property anxiety, youth employment, and the fear that technology only helps those already ahead.

This topic translates those debates for English readers without flattening them into sentiment. The goal is to explain the social mood, the historical analogies Chinese writers use, and the structural questions behind them.

## Start here

[Population, Welfare, and Housing in China's 15th Five-Year Plan](/china-society/population-welfare-15th-five-year-plan/) explains how demographics, jobs, education, healthcare, pensions, housing, and public services become part of China's growth model.

[Culture, Tourism, and Soft Power in China's 15th Five-Year Plan](/china-society/culture-soft-power-15th-five-year-plan/) explains why culture in the 2026-2030 plan is also public service, creative industry, tourism, digital content, heritage, and international communication.

[Is Upward Mobility Still Possible in the AI Age?](/china-society/upward-mobility-ai-age/) reads a Chinese essay on 300 years of wealth anxiety and asks why the argument resonates in today’s Chinese internet context.
